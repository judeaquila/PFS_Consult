Food Handling & Storage

Cold Food Temperature Control
Cold potentially hazardous foods held at 41°F (5°C) or below
Hot Food Temperature Control
Hot potentially hazardous foods held at 135°F (57°C) or above
Food Storage Practices
Raw foods stored separately from ready-to-eat foods, proper FIFO rotation
Food Thawing Procedures
Proper thawing methods used (refrigerator, cold water, microwave, cooking)
Date Marking & Labeling
Ready-to-eat foods properly dated and labeled with expiration dates

Personal Hygiene & Staff Practices

Hand Washing Compliance
Staff washing hands frequently, especially after handling raw foods
Proper Use of Gloves
Food handlers using gloves correctly and changing when contaminated
Staff Uniform & Cleanliness
Clean uniforms, hair restraints, minimal jewelry, no eating in prep areas
Employee Health Policies
Sick employee policy in place, no ill staff handling food
Food Safety Training
Staff demonstrate knowledge of food safety practices and procedures

Kitchen & Equipment Sanitation

Equipment Cleanliness
All food contact surfaces clean and sanitized between uses
Cutting Board Safety
Separate cutting boards for raw meats and ready-to-eat foods
Sanitizer Concentration
Sanitizing solutions at proper concentrations (test strips used)
Dishwashing Procedures
Three-compartment sink or dishwasher operating at proper temperatures
Kitchen Surface Cleanliness
Work surfaces, walls, and floors clean and in good repair

Dining Area & Customer Safety

Table & Chair Sanitation
Tables and chairs cleaned and sanitized between customers
Utensil & Tableware Cleanliness
Clean utensils, plates, and glasses provided to customers
Customer Restroom Facilities
Clean restrooms with soap, towels, and hot water available
Dining Area Cleanliness
Floor, walls, and ceiling clean and well-maintained
Adequate Lighting & Ventilation
Proper lighting and air circulation in dining areas

Pest Control & Waste Management

Pest Prevention Measures
No evidence of rodents, insects, or other pests
Exterior Entry Points
Doors, windows, and vents properly sealed to prevent pest entry
Waste Disposal Practices
Garbage containers covered, emptied regularly, and kept clean
Grease Trap Maintenance
Grease traps cleaned regularly and functioning properly
Storage Area Organization
Storage areas clean, organized, and off the floor

Water & Plumbing Systems

Water Source Quality
Potable water from approved source for all food operations
Hot Water Temperature
Hot water available at minimum 100°F (38°C) for hand washing
Plumbing System Integrity
No cross-connections, backflow prevention devices in place
Sewage Disposal
Sewage and wastewater disposed of through approved system
Hand Washing Facilities
Adequate hand washing sinks with soap and paper towels
